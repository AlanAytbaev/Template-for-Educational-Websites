Three Main Categories

Chemical bonds are grouped into three categories that each impact the properties of the substance that they form. Metallic bonds give metal its' characteristic luster, malleability, ductility, strength, and conductivity.

Covalent bonds, and their two subcategories, decide the intermolecular attractive forces, and therefore the boiling and melting point, solubility, and surface-tension of substances.

Ionic bonds are strong, yet short-ranged, giving the substances they create, mainly crystals, a brittle quality. Ionic bonds are an extreme example of polar covalent bonding, while metallic bonds suffice the other extreme of nonpolar covalent bonding and sharing of electrons amongst a substance.
